About Me

My name is Sabin Sidney, and I am excited to begin my education journey with everyone in this class. I was born in Bulgaria but adopted by my American parents and raised outside of Milwaukee, Wisconsin. I graduated from Wake Forest University in Winston-Salem, NC, with a B.S. in Finance. After my undergraduate degree, I moved to Washington, D.C. for three years before moving to Tallahassee almost two years ago in January 2021.

Career Goals

My career began with an unpaid internship on Capitol Hill. However, during those pivotal three years in Washington, I was fortunate to experience a vast range of exposure to American politics. For example, I had the opportunity to work in the communications shop at the Republican Attorneys General Association during the 2018 midterm cycle. After that, I served at the U.S. Department of Labor, even in the height of the COVID-19 pandemic. I have since served in the Florida Legislature and the Governor's Executive Office, working closely with high-ranking elected officials and members of the Governor's staff. I am now working privately for a federal consulting firm working on communications. However, my goal after completing the Master's degree is to work on a 2024 campaign and ultimately return to government service, hopefully in a Presidential Administration.
